History

The Miracle of Istanbul (2005): Liverpool's finest hour in the 2000s came in the 2005 UEFA Champions League final against AC Milan in Istanbul. Trailing 3-0 at halftime, Liverpool mounted one of the most famous comebacks in football history, drawing 3-3 and eventually winning on penalties. This victory marked Liverpool's 5th European Cup win, a feat that secured the club's place as one of Europe's elite teams.

Near Misses: Despite the 2005 triumph, Liverpool's domestic league form was inconsistent. They finished second in the Premier League in 2009 but were unable to win the league title during this period.

Jürgen Klopp Appointment (2015): In 2015, Liverpool appointed Jürgen Klopp as manager, ushering in a new era for the club. Klopp's arrival brought a focus on high-intensity, pressing football, famously known as "gegenpressing". Klopp transformed Liverpool into a team that played with attacking verve and resilience.

2018-2019: Champions League Glory: In 2019, Liverpool won their sixth European Cup, defeating Tottenham Hotspur 2-0 in the final. The win followed a heartbreaking defeat in the final the previous year (2018), where they lost to Real Madrid. The 2019 victory further cemented Klopp's legacy as a world-class manager.

Premier League Triumph (2020): In 2020, after a 30-year wait, Liverpool finally won the Premier League title under Klopp. Liverpool's record-breaking run, finishing with 99 points, was a testament to their dominance throughout the season. This victory ended the club's long league title drought and was seen as one of the greatest achievements in Liverpool's storied history.

The 2020s and Continued Success: Liverpool continued to be a major force in both domestic and international football throughout the 2020s, winning additional trophies like the UEFA Super Cup and FIFA Club World Cup in 2019. They also reached the 2022 FA Cup and League Cup finals, winning both competitions. Under Klopp, the club's philosophy of exciting, high-pressing football has made them one of the most entertaining and successful teams in Europe.

The Reds dominated early on, eventually capitalising as Mo Salah ran clear on the counter and Darwin Nunez collected the ball to fire past Emi Martinez. The second half was a far less eventful affair, with Liverpool dominating possesion but both sides finding it difficult to carve out clear-cut chances. As Villa chased an equaliser, Salah chased a clearance and came off better, with the ball defelcting into his path to take him one-v-one by the halfway line.
