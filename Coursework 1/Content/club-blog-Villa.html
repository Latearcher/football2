History

Managerial Change and Stability: The early 2000s saw Villa begin a process of rebuilding under manager David O'Leary, who helped bring in some exciting talent to the squad. However, the club failed to challenge for titles, with Villa consistently finishing mid-table in the Premier League.

1990s-2000s: Villa spent most of the late 1990s and 2000s in mid-table, with highlights including reaching the FA Cup final in 2000, though they were ultimately defeated by Chelsea.

2000-2009: Villa were seen as a solid top-flight side but struggled to break into the upper echelon of English football, finishing consistently in mid-table.

Relegation and Return to the Premier League: Aston Villa's fortunes took a serious downturn in the 2015-16 season when they were relegated from the Premier League after 28 years of top-flight football. This was a major shock to the club, given its history and stature in English football.

Promotion to the Premier League (2019): Villa bounced back from relegation in the 2018-19 Championship season, winning promotion via the playoffs under manager Dean Smith.

Recent Managerial Changes: After a season of mixed results in the Premier League, Unai Emery was appointed manager in 2022, bringing fresh optimism. Emery led the club to European qualification in the 2023-2024 season, marking a return to European competition for the first time in several years.
