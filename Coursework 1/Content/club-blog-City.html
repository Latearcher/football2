History

In 2008, Manchester City underwent a radical transformation. The club was bought by the Abu Dhabi United Group, led by Sheikh Mansour bin Zayed Al Nahyan, which ushered in a new era of unprecedented financial backing and ambition. The influx of investment allowed City to sign top international talent and revamp the club's infrastructure.

Under the guidance of manager Roberto Mancini, Manchester City won their first Premier League title in 44 years in 2012 in one of the most dramatic finishes to a season in football history. City secured the title in the final minutes of the last game of the season, thanks to a memorable last-minute goal by Sergio Agüero—a moment now etched in football lore.

The club continued to rise under Manuel Pellegrini and later, Pep Guardiola, who took over as manager in 2016. Guardiola led the team to new heights, securing multiple Premier League titles, including the 2017-2018 season when they amassed a record 100 points, the most ever in a Premier League season.

Since Guardiola's arrival, Manchester City has dominated English football, winning multiple Premier League titles, FA Cups, and League Cups. Under Guardiola, the team became known for their exceptional possession-based style of play, marked by intricate passing and attacking brilliance. The club also achieved back-to-back league titles in the 2018-2019 and 2020-2021 seasons.

In 2023, Manchester City reached the pinnacle of European football by winning their first-ever UEFA Champions League title. This achievement, coupled with their Premier League and FA Cup victories, completed a historic treble for the club, solidifying their status as one of the best clubs in Europe.
