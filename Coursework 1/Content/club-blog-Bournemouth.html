History

Eddie Howe's Impact: The most pivotal figure in Bournemouth's modern history has been Eddie Howe, who became manager in 2008. Howe, a former player for the club, took over when the team was in financial trouble and helped stabilize the club.

2013-14: Bournemouth reached the Championship under Eddie Howe’s leadership for the first time in their history, finishing 2nd in League One.

2014-2015: Premier League Promotion: In one of the most remarkable achievements in their history, Bournemouth secured promotion to the Premier League for the first time in the club's history at the end of the 2014-15 season. The team finished 1st in the Championship, with Howe's young team playing an exciting, attacking brand of football.

Premier League: Bournemouth spent five seasons in the Premier League, from 2015 to 2020. During their time in the top flight, the club established itself as a competitive and resilient side under Eddie Howe’s leadership, despite operating with a much smaller budget than the top clubs. The team’s high-point was finishing 9th in the Premier League in the 2016-17 season.

However, despite Howe’s success in keeping the club competitive in the Premier League, they struggled in the later seasons, eventually being relegated back to the Championship in 2020.

After their relegation in 2020, Eddie Howe left the club and was replaced by Jason Tindall, a former assistant to Howe. However, the team struggled and Tindall was replaced mid-season by Jonathan Woodgate.

In the 2021-2022 season, Bournemouth bounced back to the Premier League under Scott Parker, securing promotion via the Championship playoffs.

2022-2023: Bournemouth faced significant challenges in the Premier League but showed resilience under Parker’s leadership. After a difficult start to the 2022-23 season, Parker was sacked and replaced by Gary O'Neil, who steered the club to survival in the Premier League. Bournemouth finished 15th in the league that season, avoiding relegation.
